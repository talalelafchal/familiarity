<p>I started with apple swift today (as many others ;)) and I struggle with reading a plist.</p> <p>I read a plist entry from my info.plist which is an NSArray where each entry is a Dictionary.</p> <pre><code>let regionsToMonitor = NSBundle.mainBundle().infoDictionary["Regions"] as Array&lt;Dictionary&lt;String,AnyObject&gt;&gt; for regionToMonitor in regionsToMonitor { ... </code></pre> <p>one key for each region is a major. in objC terms a NSNumber. I read it as:</p> <pre><code>let major: Int? = regionToMonitor["major"] ? regionToMonitor["major"]!.integerValue : nil; </code></pre> <p>now I need it as a CLBeaconMajorValue</p> <p>so I try to cast it to that with <code>as</code></p> <pre><code> clRegion = CLBeaconRegion(proximityUUID: uuid, major: major! as CLBeaconMajorValue, identifier: identifier) </code></pre> <p>doesn't work. the UUID and identifier do work (Ive checked with another constructor, but the not the init</p> <p>this is also not good</p> <pre><code> let m = major! as CLBeaconMajorValue </code></pre> <p>how do I convert my NSNumber / int? to the CLBeaconMajorValue</p>
<p>You have to cast the value.</p> <p>Since <code>CLBeaconMajorValue</code> is a <code>UInt16</code> <code>typealias</code>, you can do it like this:</p> <pre><code>let m = UInt16(major!) </code></pre> <p>You can even do this:</p> <pre><code>let m = CLBeaconMajorValue(major!) </code></pre>
