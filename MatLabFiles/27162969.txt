<p>From Matlab manual</p> <blockquote> <p>y = awgn(x,snr) adds white Gaussian noise to the vector signal x. The <strong>scalar</strong> snr specifies the signal-to-noise ratio per sample, in dB.</p> </blockquote>
<p>Your vectors don't have the same size. <code>S</code> is 1x2001 and <code>W</code> is 1x2500. Try</p> <pre><code>W = M + sqrt(V)*rand(size(S)); </code></pre> <p>Then you can just add the signals by</p> <pre><code>SW = S + W; </code></pre> <p>As Kostya already wrote, <code>awgn</code> can be used if you know the desired SNR.</p>
