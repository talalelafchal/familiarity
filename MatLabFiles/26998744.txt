<p>As I mentioned in my comment, I don't think there's a way to change the command line output. If you don't need a programmatic solution you can utilize the <a href="http://www.mathworks.com/help/matlab/matlab_env/view-edit-and-copy-variables.html#btzcjxw" rel="nofollow">variable explorer</a> to interact with your data using a slightly Excel-ish interface. </p> <p>You can access the variable explorer by double clicking on your variable in the workspace browser, right clicking on your variable and selecting <code>Open</code>, selecting your variable and hitting <code>ctrl+D</code> (on Windows), or programmatically using <a href="http://www.mathworks.com/help/matlab/ref/openvar.html" rel="nofollow"><code>openvar</code></a>.</p> <p>If you do need a programmatic solution, you can use one of the many exporting functions (<a href="http://www.mathworks.com/help/matlab/ref/sprintf.html" rel="nofollow"><code>sprintf</code></a>, <a href="http://www.mathworks.com/help/matlab/ref/fprintf.html" rel="nofollow"><code>fprintf</code></a>, <a href="http://www.mathworks.com/help/matlab/ref/save.html" rel="nofollow"><code>save</code></a>, etc.), one example being the answer that @badjr posted.</p>
