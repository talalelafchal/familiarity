sorting matrix in matlab based on another vector
<p>I have a 2D matrix and want to sort rows and columns based on two other vectors i.e. one for ordering rows another for ordering columns in MATLAB</p> <p>Example: A (Matrix to order)</p> <pre><code>0 1 2 3 4 1 1 8 9 7 2 3 4 6 2 3 1 2 0 8 </code></pre> <p>Row Vector (Order for sorting rows of matrix A)</p> <pre><code>1 4 2 3 </code></pre> <p>And column vector</p> <pre><code>1 5 4 2 3 </code></pre> <p>Modified A</p> <pre><code>0 4 3 1 2 3 8 0 1 2 1 7 9 1 8 2 2 6 3 4 </code></pre>
<p>How about:</p> <pre><code>ModifiedA=A(RowVector,ColumnVector); </code></pre> <p><strong>Note</strong>: Matab's indexing starts at <code>1</code> not at <code>0</code>, adapt your indexing vectors accordingly.</p>
<p>In MATLAB, you can use the second output of <code>sort</code> to get the 1-based indexes that MATLAB is looking for (in this case you could have just added 1, but using <code>sort</code> works even if the row and column vectors are not consecutive).</p> <pre><code>[~,rowIdx] = sort(rowVector); [~,colIdx] = sort(colVector); </code></pre> <p>And then you can apply the indexing operation to the matrix:</p> <pre><code>modifiedA = A(rowIdx, colIdx); </code></pre>
