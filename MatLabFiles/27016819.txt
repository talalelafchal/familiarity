<p>Have you read the documentation of <a href="http://www.mathworks.com/help/matlab/ref/convhull.html" rel="nofollow"><code>convhull</code></a>?</p> <p>It clearly explains (for the 3D case):</p> <blockquote> <p><code>K = convhull(X,Y,Z</code>) returns the 3-D convex hull of the points (X,Y,Z), where X, Y, and Z are column vectors.<br> K is a triangulation representing the boundary of the convex hull. K is of size mtri-by-3, where mtri is the number of triangular facets. That is, each row of K is a triangle defined in terms of the point indices.</p> </blockquote> <p>In your example the convex hull of the the 4 points in <code>A</code> is defined by the four triangular facets</p> <pre><code>[1 2 4] --&gt; [ (2,0,0), (0,2,0), (1,1,sqrt(2)) ] %// first triangle [1 3 2] --&gt; [ (2,0,0), (0,0,2), (0,2,0) ] %// second triangle ... </code></pre>
