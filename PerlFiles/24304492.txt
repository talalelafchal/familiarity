<p>Check the text of your cgi script to see whether it uses unix line endings "\n" or dos/windows line endings "\r\n".</p> <p>You can do this from the shell:</p> <pre><code> $ od -c mycgiscript.pl </code></pre> <p>If you find your file riddled with windows line endings, then to this:</p> <pre><code> $ tr -d '\r' mycgiscript.pl &gt; /tmp/mycgiscript.pl $ cp /tmp/mycgiscript.pl mycgiscript.pl $ rm /tmp/mycgiscript.pl </code></pre> <p>Make sure read/execute bits don't get reset, and the try running your script again.</p>
<blockquote> <p>502 Bad gateway The CGI was not CGI/1.1 compliant</p> </blockquote> <p>Usually this error comes due to permissions. Try to add read and execute access rights to others.</p> <pre><code>chmod o+rx yourscript.cgi </code></pre>
