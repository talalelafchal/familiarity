Indexing a list returned by a subroutine - Syntax
<p>Given I have the following incorrect code:</p> <pre><code>my $this_is_easier = caller(0)[0]; </code></pre> <p>Why is it necessary for me to do the following in order to compile?</p> <pre><code>my $this_is_easier = (caller(0))[0]; </code></pre> <p>Is this just a nuance of Perl's parser? In other languages like Python and C++ it's assumed that the function will return something indexable.</p>
<p>When <code>[]</code> follows another index, it implies a dereference.</p> <pre><code>$foo[...][...] means $foo[...]-&gt;[...] </code></pre> <p>When <code>()</code> follows another index, it implies a dereference.</p> <pre><code>$foo[...](...) means $foo[...]-&gt;(...) </code></pre> <p>As such, it's very reasonable for people to expect</p> <pre><code>foo(...)[...] </code></pre> <p>to mean</p> <pre><code>foo(...)-&gt;[...] </code></pre> <p>rather than </p> <pre><code>( foo(...) )[0] </code></pre> <p>That divergence in expectations is probably the reason it's not supported.</p>
