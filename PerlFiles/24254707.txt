My Perl split function is returning individual numbers and characters. How can I fix this?
<p>I've got a character string that looks like this:</p> <p><code>803|804</code> and <code>7|7</code></p> <p>This is my PERL code:</p> <p><code>my @vpid = split /|/, $vpid; my @quantity = split('|', $quantity);</code></p> <p>My results are:</p> <p><code>8 0 3 | 8 0 4</code> and <code>7 | 7</code>.</p> <p>The results I'm looking for are <code>803 804</code> and <code>7 7</code> as the content of the arrays.</p> <p>Why is this happening and how can I fix it?</p>
<p><code>|</code> is a regex special character denoting "or". You must escape it.</p> <pre><code>my @vpid = split /\|/, '803|804'; my @quantity = split '\|', '7|7'; use Data::Dump; dd @vpid; dd @quantity; </code></pre> <p>Outputs:</p> <pre><code>(803, 804) (7, 7) </code></pre>
