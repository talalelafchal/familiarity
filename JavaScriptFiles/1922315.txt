<p>I'm not sure what your script intends to do, but for Javascript effects, check out the frameworks: </p> <p><a href="http://jquery.com/" rel="nofollow noreferrer">JQUery</a></p> <p><a href="http://www.prototypejs.org" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Prototype</a> / <a href="http://script.aculo.us" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Scriptaculous</a></p> <p><a href="http://www.mootools.net" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Mootools</a></p> <p>they have worked out most cross browser issues and are very easy to use.</p>
<p>scrollTop behaviour in IE is extremely dodgy. Two things can affect it:</p> <p>1) The document type - you have to get the right one - there appears to be some issues with transitional and loose DTDs.</p> <p>2) Whether or not there is overflow set on the container.</p> <p>See <a href="http://forums.digitalpoint.com/showthread.php?t=11965" rel="nofollow noreferrer">http://forums.digitalpoint.com/showthread.php?t=11965</a>. It presents a solution to both issues of scrollTop in IE.</p> <p>(At the risk of enacting a stackoverflow cliche, you might want to consider using a framework such as <a href="http://jquery.com/" rel="nofollow noreferrer">jQuery</a>, which will take a lot of such annoyances out of the equation).</p>
<p>You may want to set speed to a higher number.</p> <p>The second argument to <code>setInterval</code> is the delay in milliseconds. 60 milliseconds is quite fast; 50 milliseconds is 1/20th of a second.</p> <p>Maybe try 100 or 200 to see if things improve?</p>
